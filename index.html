About

The Cosme Lab at UC Riverside is validating cost-effective linked-read sequencing method to generate phased haplotypes for tracking insecticide resistance evolution in California mosquito populations (PacVec Training Grant). Our lab is approved by UCR EHS for vector competence bioassays, enabling us to investigate cellular mechanisms of dengue resistance using single-cell RNA-seq approaches (Regents Fellowship). Beyond mosquitoes, we're developing a robotic vertical farming prototype for sustainable mealworm protein production (Delfino Innovation Grant).

Through these integrated approaches, we aim to understand how disease vectors rapidly evolve and adapt, providing genomic tools that directly inform vector control strategies while pioneering sustainable protein alternatives.

Why We Use Containers

A Personal Journey to Reproducible Science

In 2024, my life changed dramatically. I left my position in Connecticut, accepted a new role at UC Riverside, and moved across the country to California. Amidst this transition, I submitted a manuscript on diapause GWAS in Aedes albopictus. When the reviews came back requesting additional analyses, I tried to replicate my results.

What followed was the scariest week of my career. My new MacBook with its ARM64 chip couldn't reproduce the analyses I had run just months earlier. Dependencies failed, versions conflicted, and results wouldn't match. I frantically tried everything until, finally, I managed to replicate the work on the HPC cluster. The relief was immense, but the computational reproducibility needed an update.

Starting January 2025, I invested three months developing containerized workflows. Now, all Cosme Lab publications include GHCR/Docker containers with complete computational environments, ensuring that anyone, anywhere, can replicate our work. For each project, I create two containers: one with all the tools to replicate the analyses on the HPC environment, and another that can run on any architecture to reproduce the figures and tables.

Our Commitment

  • Every publication includes Docker/GHCR containers
  • Complete computational environments preserved
  • Lightweight containers for figures and tables
  • Overleaf integration for seamless collaboration
  • All code and data are publicly available

"Public fund results should be reproducible by anyone, anywhere, anytime."
